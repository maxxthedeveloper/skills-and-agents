# Procrastination Framework

Four complementary theories for understanding procrastination: habit interruption, neurochemistry, identity mechanics, and state change.

## Theory 1: The Habit Loop (Mel Robbins)

Procrastination is a stress response, not a character flaw. It follows a habit loop:

1. **Trigger** — background stress (not necessarily related to the task)
2. **Pattern** — avoidance (the brain seeks relief)
3. **Reward** — temporary stress relief

### Intervention: Interrupt the Pattern
1. **Notice** the hesitation — catch yourself avoiding
2. **Acknowledge** the stress: "I must be stressed about something"
3. **Countdown** 5-4-3-2-1 — interrupts the habit, activates prefrontal cortex
4. **Start for 5 minutes** — the problem isn't working, it's starting

**Key stat:** If you start, 80% of the time you keep going.

## Theory 2: The Dopamine Trough (Andrew Huberman)

Procrastination is a neurochemical low-motivation state — a dopamine trough.

### Key Concepts
- **Limbic friction**: resistance between emotional brain and prefrontal cortex
  - Tired/unmotivated → need to energize into action
  - Anxious/scared → need to calm enough to lean forward
- Easy tasks don't create enough contrast to trigger dopamine rebound
- Waiting for motivation or relying on deadline panic is slow and unreliable

### Intervention: Steepen the Trough
Do something **harder and more uncomfortable** than the current state:
- Cold water on face / cold shower — spikes dopamine for hours
- Short burst of exercise (1-5 minutes)
- The **cringe test**: if you cringe thinking about doing it, it's the right tool

Don't wait. Don't take the easy path. Make it worse (safely) to rebound faster.

## Theory 3: Psycho-Cybernetic Blocks (Maxwell Maltz)

When procrastination is recurring despite good strategies, the block is at the identity level.

### The Servo-Mechanism
The mind is a goal-seeking system. It faithfully pursues whatever target it's given. When stuck:
- The target may not be vivid enough (especially for ADHD brains)
- The self-image may not include being someone who achieves this
- Over-trying jams the mechanism ("purpose tremor")

### The Self-Image Thermostat
People don't rise to goals — they fall to their self-image. Signs the thermostat is the block:
- Repeated start-stop cycles on the same task/project
- "I know what to do, I just can't do it"
- Procrastination that resists all systems and hacks
- Success followed by immediate regression

### Brief Identity Interventions (for /unblock — deeper work goes to /reflect)
1. Name the ceiling: "Your self-image has a wall right here."
2. ONE dehypnotization question: "When did you first learn you couldn't do this?"
3. Reframe: "What would the person who naturally does this do right now? Just the next step."

## Theory 4: State Change & The Triad (Tony Robbins)

Core insight: you don't change your mind first, then move. You move first, and your mind follows.

### The Triad
Three forces that determine your state at any moment:

1. **Physiology** (fastest lever) — movement, breathing, posture. Change any of these and your emotional state shifts instantly. Stand up, move, shake, do jumping jacks. This is always step one.
2. **Focus** — where focus goes, energy flows. Procrastination often means focus is on the pile, the overwhelm, or the fear. Redirect focus to ONE specific outcome.
3. **Language** — how you talk to yourself about the task. "I have to" vs "I get to." "This is impossible" vs "What's the first piece?"

### Pattern Interrupt
Physical disruption breaks the procrastination trigger loop. The avoidance cycle is: trigger → stress → habit (avoid) → relief. A physical interrupt (standing up, changing rooms, cold water, movement) disrupts the loop before the habit fires. Must be physical, not mental — thinking your way out of an avoidance loop doesn't work.

### RPM (Results, Purpose, Massive Action Plan)
- **Results:** What specifically does DONE look like? One sentence.
- **Purpose:** Why does this matter emotionally to YOU? Not the deadline, not the obligation — what's in it for you?
- **Massive Action Plan:** 3 chunks max. Brain handles 3 meaningful groups, not 10 line items. Each chunk has a clear outcome.

### Pain-Pleasure Leverage (Quick Dickens Process)
For recurring blocks, amplify the emotional stakes in 30 seconds:
- Pain: "If you keep avoiding this kind of work for 6 months — what does that cost you?" (10 sec)
- More pain: "What about a year?" (10 sec)
- Pleasure: "Now — what opens up if you break through it today?" (10 sec)
Not the full Dickens visualization. Just enough to create leverage for action right now.

### Chunking Rule of 3
Don't break a task into 10 steps — still overwhelming. Group into 3 meaningful chunks with clear outcomes. Each chunk should feel like a complete unit of progress.

## ADHD-Specific Considerations

- **Chaos is signal**: scattered energy means the target isn't vivid enough
- **Novelty is fuel**: boredom with a task = the goal may not be truly theirs, or it needs reframing
- **Hyperfocus = mechanism locked on**: when it doesn't happen, the target needs work, not discipline
- **"Try harder" jams the mechanism**: alignment produces more output than discipline ever will
- **Time blindness**: make the next action immediate and concrete, not "by Friday"
- **Shame spirals**: never add shame when they've been avoiding. Separate the miss from identity.

## Diagnostic Quick-Reference

| Block Type | Signal | Root Cause | Intervention | Robbins Tool |
|------------|--------|------------|--------------|--------------|
| A: Too Big/Vague | "Don't know where to start" | No clear target for servo-mechanism | Break down, create micro-tasks, schedule now | RPM (Result + Purpose + 3 chunks) |
| B: Scattered | "Too many things" / can't pick | No single vivid target | Force pick ONE, clear the rest, schedule | Focus leg of Triad |
| C: Avoidance Loop | Busy with wrong things | Stress habit loop (Robbins) | Name it, physical interrupt, 5-4-3-2-1, start 5 min | Pattern Interrupt + Physiology |
| D: Low Energy | "Can't get going" / sluggish | Dopamine trough (Huberman) | State change FIRST, then micro-task, schedule | Physiology leg of Triad |
| E: Identity Block | Recurring avoidance, high stakes | Self-image thermostat (Maltz) | Quick Dickens, dehypnotize, smallest version, deeper in /reflect | Pain-Pleasure Leverage |
